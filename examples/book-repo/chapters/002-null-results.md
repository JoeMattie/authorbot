<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc3-93e0-7628-b538-d53c5022e1de" -->
Theo Abara believed in the drift the way he believed in weather forecasts, which is to say he wanted better data before canceling the picnic.

<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc4-7e40-724d-be00-a1c65f69ea13" -->
So they set out to kill the signal. They swapped the reference laser, the timing card, the fiber run, and, over one long Thursday, the entire acquisition chain; they moved the analysis onto Theo's own machines and rewrote the pipeline from its import statements upward.

<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc5-68a0-73c3-acbc-73cdc001dbb9" -->
The arc survived every substitution. It kept its seventy-one minutes with the indifference of a tide.

<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc6-5300-7c61-b8e3-80de6952420f" -->
The night they ran the annex on generator power, isolated from the grid and from every clock but its own, Mara stayed to watch the residuals compile. The log entry from that run is short:

<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc7-3d60-7bd9-a41e-6fc59661435d" -->
```text
run 47 | cycle 71.0 min | amp 4.1e-4 | phase stable
grid: isolated | ref: cesium-2 | pipeline: theo-rewrite
conclusion: not us
```

<!-- authorbot:block id="019d0bc8-27c0-7913-8d7f-5c68d2b54c4c" -->
Theo ran the totals a third time, not because he doubted the first two passes, but because the alternative was admitting that the interferometer was telling the truth.
